Frequently Asked Questions

My approach to work, leadership, and strategy.

Tell me about yourself.

I'm a strategic technology leader who gets results by bridging the gap between technical possibilities and business goals. I thrive on making complex technology simple and delivering measurable value, whether that's through financial governance, robust security architecture, or building empowered, high-performing teams.

What makes you stand out?

My ability to operate at both the 30,000-foot strategic level and the 3-foot delivery level. I can architect a multi-year Zero Trust strategy and, in the same day, work with my engineering team to unblock a critical Terraform script. This blend of executive vision and deep technical credibility allows me to lead effectively and earn the trust of both the boardroom and the server room.

Can you share a major professional achievement?

Situation: When I joined the council, cloud spending was reactive and lacked centralized oversight, leading to budget overruns and inconsistent security practices.
Task: My goal was to establish a culture of financial accountability and strategic governance for our entire cloud estate.
Action: I researched, proposed, and pioneered the adoption of a FinOps methodology. This involved creating clear Power BI reports for finance, establishing a cross-departmental Azure Governance Board which I now chair, and embedding cost-awareness into our project lifecycle.
Result: We cut our annual cloud bill by 15% within six months, eliminated surprise costs, and now make all cloud investment decisions based on clear data and business alignment.

What are your key strengths and areas for improvement?

My key strength is strategic problem-solving—I excel at untangling complex technical and business challenges and creating a clear, actionable path forward. I'm also very effective at managing stakeholder expectations and communicating the 'why' behind technical decisions. An area I'm actively focused on improving is delegation; with my hands-on background, my instinct is often to jump in and help solve a problem directly. I'm consciously working on stepping back more, empowering my team to find the solution, and focusing my energy on the higher-level strategic blockers only I can solve.

What motivates you?

I'm motivated by tangible impact. I love the challenge of taking a chaotic system or a vague business problem and creating an elegant, efficient, and secure solution that makes people's jobs easier and saves the organization money. Seeing my team succeed and grow their skills is also a huge driver for me. What I enjoy most about my current role is the variety—one day I'm deep in a DDI strategy review, the next I'm helping the team navigate a complex data migration, and the day after I'm presenting financial reports to leadership. The main challenge is always balancing urgent, tactical needs with long-term strategic goals, which is a puzzle I enjoy solving.

What is your notice period and location preference?

My current notice period is three months. I am based in Manchester and am ideally looking for remote or hybrid roles. I am open to hybrid work in the Manchester area.
